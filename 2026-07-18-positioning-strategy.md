## 1. The one-sentence positioning statement

**"A church history teacher who builds practical AI systems — for people who want depth, tools that work, and communities worth living in."**

The load-bearing choice: **teacher is the noun; builder is the verb; real estate is a context, not an identity.** The current hero already says this in three fragments. Own it as one sentence and stop re-litigating it. Every future "should I add X to the site" question resolves against this: does X show teaching depth, working tools, or community stewardship? If not, it doesn't go on the site.

## 2. Audiences and their tensions

**Primary: academic/ministry gatekeepers** — APTS colleagues, seminary admins, endorsers (cf. Request-Endorsement-Solomon-Wang), donors, prospective institutions. They arrive via HT101 or your name. They must find: a serious teacher, doctrinal steadiness, no whiff of hustle.

**Secondary A: AI-curious educators and knowledge workers** — people who find Graphify/vault work interesting. They validate the "builder" claim and are the most likely Book With Me bookers.

**Secondary B: real-estate/coliving operators** — Skool/Andres world, PadSplit-adjacent. They need one page proving you're a real operator, then move to conversation off-site.

**The tensions, named:**
- *Donor vs. operator:* a donor who sees "investor" leads reads everything else as funding a side hustle. Resolution (already half-implemented): real estate speaks only in stewardship/community language on the site; deal language lives in conversations, never on pages.
- *Academic vs. AI-builder:* mild and actually productive — "professor who ships tools" is a differentiator, not a conflict. Don't hide either from the other.
- *Book With Me ambiguity:* "thoughtful conversations" is vague enough to protect the ministry frame but weak for conversion. Accept the trade-off for the next 90 days: credibility > conversion. Do not add pricing/service tiers to the public site yet.

## 3. Homepage information hierarchy (confirmed, one change)

Keep exactly: Hero → four cards (HT101, AI Projects, Real Estate, Book with Me) → footer. This order is correct: teaching first, tools second, community third, invitation last.

One change: the hero CTA pair currently leads with "Let's talk" (/book). For the primary audience, **"About Ang" should be primary and "Let's talk" secondary** — gatekeepers want to verify before they contact, and a talk-first CTA reads faintly commercial. One-line Codex swap.

## 4. What belongs on the site — now / later / never

**Now (keep, deepen):** HT101 page (flagship — fix the SHIP-AUDIT P0/P1 content items first), AI Projects (Graphify, vault automation, teaching tools), Real Estate in current stewardship framing, Book With Me, About.

**Later (90-day+ parking lot):** a written-artifacts section (one HT101-derived essay or one AI-workflow writeup — only when one exists and is genuinely good); student outcomes/testimonials from the HT101 cohort; a Graphify demo or public repo link if it ever hardens.

**Never (on this site):** deal flow, investor decks, cap rates, PadSplit economics, "work with me on real estate" funnels; AI hot-take content or model comparisons; a blog cadence you won't keep; the /today and /personal pages if they aren't maintained — **decision required: either give /today a real update ritual (Hermes cadence) or remove the route.** A stale "today" page is worse than none.

## 5. Real-estate language — use / avoid

**Use:** stewardship, housing, coliving, community, hospitality, "how people live and gather," operations, "technology-enabled stewardship" (already on the site — it's good). First person: "I pay attention to…", "I'm involved in…", never "I invest in…".

**Avoid:** investor, portfolio, deals, passive income, cash flow, units, arbitrage, "financial freedom," any dollar figures, any before/after numbers, platform names as achievements ("my PadSplit properties"). Also avoid over-explaining the ambiguity — a defensive paragraph about *why* you're interested in real estate signals exactly what it tries to hide. The current page's restraint is right.

**The litmus:** every sentence on /real-estate should be one a seminary dean could read aloud in a faculty meeting without anyone shifting in their seat — while an operator still recognizes "this person runs real properties."

## 6. 90-day priority sequence — three bets, no more

**Bet 1 (weeks 1–2): Make the flagship true.** Clear SHIP-AUDIT-2026-07-09 P0+P1 on /ht101 — stale course dates, "five worked examples" claim, placeholder card. A credibility site with wrong dates on its flagship page is net-negative. This is Codex work gated on one decision from you (actual course-date status, post-APTS).

**Bet 2 (weeks 2–8): One durable written artifact.** Publish a single substantive piece on the site — recommended: "How I taught church history with an AI-assisted pipeline" (HT101 + AI in one artifact; serves primary AND secondary-A audiences; already have the raw material in the vault). One piece, edited hard. Not a blog.

**Bet 3 (weeks 4–12): Route Book With Me traffic deliberately.** Add one sentence per section page pointing to /book with a section-specific frame ("If you're thinking about AI-assisted teaching, book a conversation"). Measure: bookings that mention a specific page. No pricing, no packages, no funnel software.

Everything else — including new sections, redesigns, SEO campaigns — is explicitly deferred.

## 7. Stop doing / do not build yet

- **Stop** treating the site as unfinished. It's shipped; only maintain and deepen.
- **Do not build:** a newsletter, a course platform (HT101 hosting stays where it is), a real-estate page expansion, a testimonial carousel, analytics beyond the basics, dark-mode/theming passes, any second domain or brand split ("separate RE site") — the split-brand instinct is the scattered-effort failure mode this document exists to prevent. One site, one person, four expressions.
- **Do not write yet:** case studies with numbers (RE or AI ROI claims). Wait until there's an artifact so strong it doesn't need numbers.

## 9. Strongest objections, and how the site answers them

**Ministry donor:** *"Is he actually committed to teaching, or is this a tech/property guy doing ministry on the side?"* — Answer structurally, not verbally: HT101 is the first card, the deepest page, and the flagship artifact (Bet 2) is about teaching. Real estate gets one restrained page with zero commercial language. The site's proportions ARE the answer; never write a paragraph protesting commitment.

**Prospective client/booker (AI or teaching):** *"Interesting person, but can he actually do the work — and what would I even book him for?"* — Answer with the artifact (Bet 2 shows the work end-to-end) and the section-specific /book framings (Bet 3 tells them what a conversation is for). Graphify/projects page carries the "real tools, not demos" burden — keep 'problem → what it does' framing, no feature lists.

**Collaborator/peer (academic or operator):** *"Is he spread too thin to be a reliable partner?"* — Answer with coherence: one positioning sentence, four expressions of one concern (formation, tools, community), visible finished work (a taught course, shipped tools, a live site). The "stop doing" list (§7) is what keeps this objection answerable — every new surface added to the site weakens it.

**Assumptions made explicit:** (a) HT101 cohort concluded ~July 2026 and teaching continues in future terms — if HT101 was one-off, Bet 2's topic shifts to the AI-pipeline story alone; (b) Book With Me remains relationship-driven, not revenue-driven, for these 90 days; (c) no institutional constraint (APTS policy) prohibits the AI or RE pages — worth a 5-minute check before Bet 2 publishes; (d) traffic is low and referral-driven, so SEO investment is correctly deferred.
